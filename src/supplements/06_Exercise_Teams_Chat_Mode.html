Why This Matters

Microsoft Teams has no built-in dictation feature. Slack's is limited. Discord doesn't have one at all. Chat vs. Email: The Key Difference

When you dictate the same thought, the destination format should shape the output. Watch what happens when the same core message goes through different modes:

The @Mention Trick

Say "at Sarah" ‚Üí Get "@Sarah"

When you want to mention someone in your message, just say "at" before their name. The AI converts your spoken "at" into a proper @mention that Teams, Slack, and Discord all recognize. This works for individual names, groups, and special mentions.

Say: "at Sarah can you review this"

‚Üí @Sarah can you review this?

Say: "at team meeting in 5"

‚Üí @team Meeting in 5!

Say: "at channel new update posted"

‚Üí @channel New update posted.

Say: "at here anyone free to help"

‚Üí @here Anyone free to help?

Why This Works

When you speak "at Sarah," SuperWhisper's transcription engine hears it as "at Sarah" (two words). The AI instruction we added specifically looks for this pattern and converts it to the @mention format that chat platforms expect. No special pronunciation needed‚Äîjust speak naturally.

Troubleshooting

Messages Too Long?

If your chat output still feels like email, make sure you removed any greetings/sign-offs from your prompt OR add this line: - Strip any formal greetings or closings the user includes

@Mentions Not Converting?

Make sure you're saying "at" as a separate word, not slurred into the name. "at Sarah" works. "atSarah" may not. Also verify the requirement line is present: Convert "at [name]" to @[name] mentions

Wrong Mode Activating?

If Email Mode is activating in Teams instead of Chat Mode, check your auto-activation rules. Teams might be matching a more general rule. Make the Teams rule more specific, or adjust the priority order in your modes list.

Pro Tip: Create Platform-Specific Variations

While one "Chat Mode" works for all platforms, power users often create separate modes for each: Teams Mode (professional), Slack Mode (tech casual + emoji suggestions), and Discord Mode (fully casual). Each has slightly different tone rules. Start with one universal mode, then branch out as you discover your preferences.
