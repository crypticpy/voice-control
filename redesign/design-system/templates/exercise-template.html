Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Open SuperWhisper Settings

~1 min

Click the SuperWhisper icon in your menu bar (Mac) or system tray (Windows), then click the gear icon or select "Settings".

✓ CHECKPOINT: You should see the Settings window with tabs on the left sidebar.
2

Navigate to Modes

~30 sec

In the Settings panel, find and click the "Modes" tab on the left sidebar.

3

Create New Custom Mode

~1 min

Click the "+ Create Custom Mode" button at the bottom of the modes list.

Name your mode: "{{MODE_NAME}}" or similar descriptive name
4

Select Your AI Models

~1 min

Scroll to the "Voice Model" and "Language Model" sections.

Voice Model: Select Fast or Standard (LOCAL)
Language Model: Select a LOCAL model (not cloud)

⚠ Important

Make sure you select LOCAL models only. Look for "LOCAL" labels or avoid models marked as "Cloud". This keeps your content on your device.

5

Configure Auto-Activation

~2 min

Scroll to "Auto-Activation Rules" and click "Add Rule".

Application: Select "{{TARGET_APP}}"
(Add additional apps as needed for your workflow)
6

Enable Context Settings

~1 min

In the "Context" section, enable these toggles:

☑ Application Context — Knows which app you're in
☑ Selected Text Context — For responding to content
7

Enter Your AI Instructions

~2 min

Scroll to the "AI Instructions" text area and paste the following prompt:

AI Instructions 
<role>
You are a professional assistant for a government health department.
</role>

<instructions>
The User Message is dictated speech. Transform it into clear,
professional output appropriate for the target application.
</instructions>

<requirements>
- Professional, appropriate tone
- Clear, concise language
- Appropriate for government workplace
- Preserve technical terms exactly as spoken
</requirements>
8

Save Your Mode

~30 sec

Click "Save" or "Done" to save your new custom mode.

✓ CHECKPOINT: Your new mode should appear in the modes list.

Completion Checklist

Troubleshooting

Mode doesn't auto-activate?

Check that {{TARGET_APP}} is listed in your auto-activation rules and that the rule is enabled. Try manually selecting the mode from the menu bar to verify it works.

Output looks like raw text?

Make sure you selected a Language Model (AI model), not just a Voice Model. The Language Model is what processes and formats your speech.

Processing seems slow?

Local models take a moment to load on first use. Try the "Fast" model if Standard is too slow. Performance improves after the first use.
